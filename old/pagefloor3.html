What floor are we on?

A white grid painted on a play field for the exhibition, 'ALL WORK NO PLAY' in Saint-Quentins chapel in Ghent. It was painted for a performance called 'What floor are we on?' that could be seen from an outdoors balcony, named after a previous exhibition in the basements of Zebrastraat buildings. The size of a single square of the grid matches the size of an iron frame used in another performance called 'The Blue Rectangle'. An extended version of this was performed with a new text read live by a male and female reader that accompagnied the viewers on the balcony. The text points out that there are three floors: the ground floor with the grid, another tiny floor centered on the grid, and the top floor balcony.
